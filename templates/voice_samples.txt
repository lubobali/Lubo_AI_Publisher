# === SAMPLE 7 — Stock Pipeline Optimization ===
Zach, the stock pipeline was brutally broken this time, real challenge. I think for a second I felt how you were feeling at Netflix, Airbnb, and Meta fixing these things in production. So here's what happened. I got a PySpark pipeline processing 1.7 million minute-level stock bars. NVDA, TSLA, AAPL, AMZN, META, MSFT, GOOGL, NFLX and more.
It calculates daily OHLCV, RSI signals, buy/sell regime classification, and cross-ticker correlation for every pair.

The pipeline worked.
The results were correct. But the performance was a disaster.

I profiled every stage, dug into the Catalyst physical plans, and found 8 anti-patterns hiding in 7 stages:

 - collect() pulling 1.7M rows to the driver just to count them
 - Re-reading the same table from storage instead of reusing the cached
 DataFrame
 - Python UDFs breaking whole-stage codegen with Pickle serialization per
 row
 - F.first("open") / F.last("close") non-deterministic without ordering
 - Broadcast joins disabled on 10-row tables forcing full shuffles
 - repartition(500) before a groupBy that already shuffles
 - Self-join with date_add() that silently skips weekends and holidays
 - 10 Exchange nodes where 4 would do

Fixed them all. 183 seconds down to 47. 4x faster.

But speed wasn't even the best part.
I caught 2 correctness bugs the original silently missed.
F.first("open") is non-deterministic inside a groupBy, and date_add(trade_date, 1) doesn't know that Friday + 1 is Monday on Wall Street. The broken version missed 268 regime changes. Just gone. Silent data loss.

Built the full visualisation notebook on Databricks with interactive Plotly charts. Candlesticks, RSI signal bands, regime change heatmaps, cross-ticker correlation matrices.
All from real marketdata, all interactive.

This is the real feel of stock market fluctuations. The data moves fast, the signals shift daily, and if your pipeline can't keep up or gets the data wrong, you're trading on lies.

Tech: PySpark, Delta Lake, Databricks Serverless, Unity Catalog, Plotly

NVIDIA NV-EmbedQA-E5-V5 vs Snowflake Arctic Embed L v2.0 - both 1024 dimensions. Both cosine similarity. So what is the difference? I built two RAG pipelines with two different embedding models and I want to share what I found.

LuBot.ai runs NVIDIA embeddings with FAISS vector search in production. 1,024-dim vectors powering 6 different features —
 - document RAG,
- intent matching,
- conversation memory,
- semantic clustering,
- few-shot RAG learning,
- and vector search.

Then I built the same thing in Snowflake. Parsed a 208-page PDF into 1,225 chunks,
 embedded with Arctic Embed,
vector search with
 0.81 similarity scores.
Same 1024 dimensions.
Same math.

 The real difference?
Not the embeddings - they are almost identical in quality.

 The difference is where they run.
Snowflake keeps everything inside their ecosystem.
 NVIDIA gives you freedom - cloud, on-premise,
 even self-hosted on a RTX 4090 which I actually do.

 Same pattern. Different platforms. Both powerful.

Sometimes you need to build the same thing twice to truly understand how powerful it is.
